# Why A ?

A was born out of personal needs. As an autistic person, I regularly find popular apps like GMail or Microsoft Office distracting. There are simply too much information, most of which I don't need. The forced updates, embedded advertisements, which are unfortunately increasingly frequent as Big tech companies that have already captured the market seek to maximise profitability, increasingly frustrate me. 

One day, I said enough is enough, and I moved onto Linux. The experience become better day at night. No more publicities, no more nasty compulsory updates degrading functionality while I am trying to focus. While the existing GNU/Linux software ecosystem provides already much, much more than a person can use in a lifetime, I still find it hard to use software designed for neurotypic people. Libre office, for example, is almost designed to imitate Microsoft office (Libre office does many things better, but still); Thunderbird, while an excellent email client, is not much less distracting than GMail (I do have habitually a full inbox, as it is the norm here in France). In short, although GNU/Linux software ecosystem drastically improved my experience, my needs can still be better answered.

So, another day, while I had an absolutely empty agenda (which is unfortunately so rare for me nowadays), I said to myself, why not write something that is from onstart designed to reduce sensory overload ? What started as a few short bash scripts eventually grew into an entire CLI/TUI program called [Autish](https://github.com/Ron-RONZZ-org/autish/). The program, although mostly a wrapper for existing GNU/Linux/Python packages, as I find absolutely zero reason to reinvent the wheel, had a much more consistent and minimalist userspace that I found extremely soothing.

The story could have ended there, except that Autish eventually grew into an ugly, giant monster. Autish was started as an one-person amateur project, with only the vague idea of reducing sensory stimulation. There is no project scope, no architectural design, no issue tracking. The development strategy was basically that every time I become adequately frustrated with existing software, I would expand autish so I don't need to touch the offending piece of software again in my life. As more and more things were added, Autish, which was originally a knight to tackle the evil monster of sensory-overload, became the monster themself. The userspace become less and less consistent, the implementations become less and less efficient. Shortly after [the first and only pre-release of autish](https://github.com/Ron-RONZZ-org/autish/releases/tag/0.0.2), I decided the project has reached an unmaintainable state.

Autish became the evil itself, but my needs are still real. With the lessons I learnt creating Autish, I started A as an Autish 2.0. I decided to not call it Autish, as although much of it is directly inspired by Autish, A is developped as a completely independent project, aspiring to fulfill the same needs, but globally much better than Autish, which means large parts of Autish was rewritten, if not from scratch, at very base level.

Also, deep down, I would rather keep Autish unique and special. With the time and energy I devoted to Autish, I grew emotionally attached to it. Yet I know clearly Autish has no future: it no longer even satisfy my own needs as an autistic person, how would it satisfy the needs of all people, neurodiverse or neurotypic, seeking to reduce sensory overload ?
